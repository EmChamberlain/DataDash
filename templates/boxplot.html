Understanding a Boxplot:

Summary: A box plot is a chart used in data analysis to visually show the distribution of numerical data and skewness through displaying the data quartiles (percentiles) and averages. They show the five-number summary of a data set partioned into four even segments:

Skew: Depending on where the median line is located on the boxplot, it will show how evenly the data is distributed. A positive skew would mean that generally students did better on the test than the median value. A negative skew would mean that students did worse on the test than the median value. A median line in the center means the median is an accurate representation of the middle test score value.

Outliers: Outliers can skew the data distribution and the mean. It can be calculated as shown below, and you can view them as a point below or above the minimum and maximum values for the boxplot. Were there any outliers on your boxplot? Those are the students who either did exceptionally well or poor on your quiz/test.
